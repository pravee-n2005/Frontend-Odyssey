01
Sunlight Zone · 0 – 200 m

The World
Above Fades

You break the surface and the noise of the world disappears. Below you, coral blooms in impossible colours. Schools of fish part around your body. You are weightless. You are a guest in someone else's world.

The Sunlight Zone extends just 200 metres — less than 0.002% of the ocean's total depth. Yet this sliver of lit water sustains 90% of all marine life on Earth, powered by photosynthesis and the sun's relentless energy.

03
Midnight Zone · 1000 – 4000 m

They Can
See You

You cannot see your hands in front of your face. Total, absolute darkness. But something out there carries its own light. Eyes adapted over millions of years to find you. Welcome to the midnight zone. Click anywhere — see what finds you.

Over 76% of deep-sea creatures produce bioluminescence. It is not decoration — it is communication, predation, camouflage. In the darkest place on Earth, light is the most powerful weapon.

04
Hadal Zone · 6000 – 11,000 m

The End
of the World

Named after Hades — god of the underworld. At 11 kilometres below the surface, the pressure is 1,100 times greater than at sea level. Your body would be crushed to the size of a fist. More humans have stood on the moon than have been here.
